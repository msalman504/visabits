Canada eTA vs Visitor Visa

UK residents have two options for visiting Canada: the Electronic Travel Authorization (eTA) for visa-exempt travelers, or a Temporary Resident Visa (TRV) for those who require one. We help you determine which option is right for you.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about Canada tourist visas

Do I need an eTA or visitor visa for Canada?

UK passport holders typically need an eTA for tourist visits. If you're from a non-exempt country or have specific circumstances, you may need a visitor visa instead.

How long can I stay in Canada with an eTA?

With an eTA, you can stay in Canada for up to 6 months per visit. The eTA itself is valid for up to 5 years, allowing multiple visits during this period.

Can I work in Canada with a tourist visa?

No, tourist visas and eTAs do not allow you to work in Canada. They are strictly for tourism, visiting family/friends, and short business meetings.

Do I need to visit an embassy for an eTA?

No, eTA applications are completed entirely online. There's no need to visit an embassy or consulate for the eTA process.

What if my application is rejected?

While our success rate is 97%, if your application is rejected, we'll help you understand the reasons and guide you through a reapplication process at no additional cost.
